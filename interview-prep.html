Interview Preparation

Preparing for your interview is not like cramming for an exam. It takes much more time to adequately prepare for the variety of subjects you may be expected to know. Your coursework will help considerably, but you also need to develop your skills as a programmer, and expose yourself to the variety of question types that are apt to be asked in the technical interview.

coding practice

There are now plenty of sites that allow you to learn and practice many languages. The most useful to the aspiring quant are Python, Java, C++.

  • HackerRank.com has some of the best tutorials. When you solve a challenging problem, be sure to review how others did it.
  • codility.com is used to test candidates (e.g. the Chicago Trading Company)
  • leetcode.com is one of the practice sites favored by comp sci students
  • I use Google + stackexchange.com all the time while coding, sometimes even if I already know how to do something. For example, what's the best way to access the last element in a Python list? You might already know how, but it's good to read how others do it.

a reading list

  • A Practical Guide to Quantitative Finance Interviews by Xinfeng Zhou.
  • 150 Most Frequently Asked Questions on Quant Interviews by Dan Stefanica, Rados Radoicic, and Tai-Ho Wang.
  • Heard on the Street: Quantitative Questions from Wall Street Job Interviews by Timothy Crack.
  • Cracking the Coding Interview by Gayle Laakmann McDowell. Gayle provides many of the online tutorials on . While it may seem that her book is focused on the computer scientists, the fact is that many of our candidates get questions just like these on the interview. Being a strong programmer is often a big competitive advantage in distinguishing yourself as a quant.
  • Python for Finance: Analyze Big Financial Data by Yves Hilpisch. This does a great job of introducing the capabilities of the numpy and pandas libraries for Python.
